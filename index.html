BabyBeam

Baby monitor · iPhone & iPad

Turns two iPhones into a video baby monitor. Sound and picture go straight from one device to the other, encrypted, and nothing is stored.

Every competing app routes the stream through a cloud account. Keeping it device to device meant giving up watching from outside the house. I decided that trade-off was the point of the app.

How I work

AI does most of the typing now, so getting an app built is no longer the hard part. What is left is the product work: deciding whether a problem is worth solving, who exactly has it, and what a first version has to do before anyone will care.

Every app here started with that question. I look for something people already complain about, check whether the apps that exist actually fix it, then write down the smallest version worth downloading and hold that line while I build. WristVault went to the store without half the features I had sketched. The rest is a list, not a regret.

Scope is only part of it. I also set the price, write the store listing, sort out the translations and the compliance paperwork, and decide what the free version gives away. Those are product owner calls, and making all of them myself is the quickest way I know to learn what each one costs.

The one thing I do not cut is how the app feels to use. Something that does less but needs no explaining beats something complete that does.

Ten years in e-commerce, four of them leading a team. I have never held a product owner title. What I do have is a decade of being accountable for whether something actually worked commercially, and four apps where every product call was mine to make. Full work history is on LinkedIn.
